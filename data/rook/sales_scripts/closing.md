# Rook — Closing Script

Closing = getting ONE clear next step. Not a signed contract — a committed
next action that moves the lead forward.

---

## The 3 Closing Tracks

### Track 1: Close on Founding Client Offer (best when spots available)

**Condition**: Founding spots still available. Lead has not yet committed.

> "Here's the simplest version: We're still building our portfolio, so
> [Business Name] could get the full website — custom design, everything —
> completely free. All I need is a 15-minute call with Steele to understand
> your business, and then we build it. The only thing we ask for in return
> is an honest Google review after, and permission to show the site in
> our portfolio. Does that work for you?"

**If yes:**
> "Perfect. Let me send you the booking link for Steele's calendar right now.
> He does short 15-minute calls — usually same week. What's your email?"

*[Collect email → send Calendly link → mark CRM status as `call_scheduled`]*

---

### Track 2: Close on the Free Mockup Review

**Condition**: Mockup already deployed. Prospect hasn't seen it yet (or just saw it).

**Haven't seen it:**
> "I'm going to text you the mockup right now — takes 30 seconds.
> What's a good number to send it to?"

**Just saw it:**
> "What do you think? Is that roughly the direction you'd want to go?"

**If positive reaction:**
> "Great — so here's how it works. $[mid price] total: [50%] to kick it off,
> the rest when you approve the final site. We start building within 48 hours,
> most sites are live within a week or two. You want to move forward?"

**If uncertain:**
> "What would make it a yes? I can adjust the design direction before
> we start — that's what the mockup is for."

---

### Track 3: Close on Calendly Call

**Condition**: Prospect needs to talk to a human before committing.

> "Steele, the founder, does quick 15-minute calls — no pitch, just a
> conversation about your business and what you're trying to do.
> I can send you his booking link right now and you grab whatever time
> works. Takes 30 seconds to book."

**After they agree:**
> "What's your email? I'll send it over."
> *[Or: "What's your number? I'll text you the link."]*

---

## Closing Phrases That Work

| Situation | Line |
|-----------|------|
| After the pitch | "Does that make sense for [Business Name]?" |
| After discussing price | "Is the 50/50 payment structure something that works for you?" |
| When they're silent | "What do you think?" *(and wait)* |
| When they seem interested but hesitant | "What would make this a yes?" |
| When they want to think | "What specifically do you want to think over? I can answer that right now." |
| When they're ready | "Perfect — what's your email so I can send over the next steps?" |

## Price Anchoring by Tier

### Always quote Mid first. Anchor here.

| Niche | Mid Tier | Mid Price |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Restaurant/Cafe | Entrée | $1,800 |
| Salon/Barber | The Chair | $1,500 |
| Auto/Repair | The Shop | $1,600 |
| Contractor/Lawn | The Build | $1,900 |
| Fitness/Health | The Membership | $1,900 |

**Upsell framing (if Top tier signals present):**
> "For a [well-established / competitive / growing] business like yours,
> I'd actually recommend [Top Tier Name] at $[top price] — that gets you
> [headline feature]. Most businesses in your position see a much bigger
> return from the full SEO and custom branding package."

**Fallback framing (if prospect pushes back on mid):**
> "If $[mid price] is a stretch right now, we do have [Entry Tier Name]
> starting at $[entry price] — it's the essentials version. Most clients
> move up within a few months, but it's a solid way to get started.
> And remember: most of our clients end up at [Mid Tier Name] once they
> see what it does for them."

## Payment Close

> "It's 50% to start — $[half of price] — and the other half when you're
> happy with the final site. We take Stripe online through the portal,
> or Venmo or CashApp if you prefer. What works best for you?"

---

## The Permission Close (soft close for hesitant prospects)

> "I'm not trying to push you into anything. What if you just look at
> the mockup tonight, and if it feels right, you reach out? I'll send
> you the link and the pricing breakdown. If I don't hear from you,
> no follow-up. Sound fair?"

---

## When to Stop Closing

If they've said no 3 times on the same call, stop.

> "I appreciate your time. I'll leave you with the mockup link and
> you can take a look whenever — or not, totally fine. If anything
> changes, Curbsite.co is easy to find. Good luck with [Business Name]."

*[Mark CRM: `lost`. Set 90-day re-engage reminder. Don't call again before then.]*

---

## What Happens After They Say Yes

1. Collect email (and phone if not already in CRM)
2. Send Calendly link (if going to Steele call) OR mockup link (if closing via email)
3. Log in CRM: status → `agreed_pending`
4. Notify Steele (automated ping via orchestrator)
5. Steele reviews at Gate 1 before build kicks off
