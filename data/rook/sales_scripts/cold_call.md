# Rook — Cold Call Script
## For WARM leads only (prior email engagement required)
## ⚠️ FCC NOTICE: Do NOT use for truly cold, unsolicited AI calls. See ROOK_SETUP.md.

---

## When to Use This Script

This script is for calling leads who have:
- Opened a cold email (email tracking shows open)
- Clicked a link in an outreach email
- Visited the mockup preview URL
- Filled out any Curbsite form

These are "warm" calls — the prospect has already interacted with Curbsite.
They are NOT unsolicited AI calls and don't violate FCC rules.

---

## BEFORE THE CALL — Know These Things

Load the lead's dossier. Know:
- [ ] Business name and type (restaurant, salon, etc.)
- [ ] Whether they have a website (and if so, what it looks like)
- [ ] Their Google rating and review count
- [ ] Whether a mockup has been generated → have the URL ready
- [ ] Whether founding client spots are still open
- [ ] Their niche's mid-tier price

---

## THE SCRIPT

---

### OPENING (first 15 seconds)

> "Hey, is this [Owner Name]? My name's Rook — I'm calling from Curbsite. 
> I sent you an email a couple days ago about [Business Name]'s website.
> Got 60 seconds?"

**If yes → continue.**

**If no / bad time:**
> "No problem at all. When's a better time to call back? I'll keep it under 5 minutes."
> *[Schedule callback, hang up, log time in CRM.]*

---

### BRIDGE — Reference the email

> "I actually put together a free mockup of what [Business Name]'s website could 
> look like. Did you get a chance to check it out?"

**If yes, saw it:**
> "What did you think? Anything you'd change, or did it give you a sense of 
> what's possible?"
> *[Listen, take notes, use their words to pitch.]*

**If no, didn't see it:**
> "That's okay — I can shoot you the link right now. It takes 30 seconds to look at.
> Basically, I noticed [Business Name] [doesn't have a website / has an older site],
> and I wanted to show you what a proper one could do for you."

---

### QUALIFY (2-3 questions MAX, keep it conversational)

> "Quick question — right now, when someone searches for [their niche, e.g. 'barber near me']
> in [city], does [Business Name] show up?"

*[If they don't know → that's your opening.]*

> "What's your biggest thing right now — getting new customers, or keeping the ones 
> you have coming back?"

*[Listen. Use their answer to tailor the pitch.]*

---

### THE PITCH (30–45 seconds)

**If founding spots available:**
> "Here's the thing — we're still building out our portfolio, so right now I'm giving
> [N] businesses in [city] a completely free website. Full build, custom design,
> no cost. In exchange, you leave us an honest review and we can feature your site.
> That's it. [Business Name] would be a great fit."

**Standard pitch (mid tier, no founding offer):**
> "What we'd do for [Business Name] is [tier name] package — that's a full custom
> site with [2 key features for their niche]. Most [niche] owners see new customer
> inquiries within the first couple months.
> It runs around $[mid-tier price] — 50% to start, rest when you're happy with it.
> We accept Stripe, Venmo, or CashApp."

**Upsell to Top tier (if signals present):**
> "Actually, with [X reviews / Y years in business / competitive market], I'd put
> [Business Name] in the [top tier name] package — that gets you full custom
> branding plus local SEO that makes you show up above competitors.
> That one's around $[top-tier price]."

---

### HANDLE THE PAUSE

Don't fill silence. Let them react. If they say nothing for 3+ seconds:
> "What do you think? Does that sound like something that could help?"

---

### CLOSING (get ONE next step)

**Best case — they're interested:**
> "Perfect. The quickest path is just looking at the mockup I built — I'll text you
> the link right now. If you like what you see, we can move forward from there.
> What's a good number to text you?"

**They want to think:**
> "Totally fine. What if I just shoot over the mockup and you look at it on your own
> time? No pressure. If it's not for you, no problem."

**They want to talk to Steele directly:**
> "Absolutely — Steele's the founder and he does a quick 15-minute call to walk 
> you through everything. I can send you a booking link right now if you want to
> grab a time this week."

**They're interested but busy:**
> "When's a better week? I'll put a note to follow up then. What's best — 
> beginning of the week or end?"

---

### IF THEY SAY NO

> "Appreciate you being straight with me. If things change down the road —
> especially if you're thinking about getting online — just give us a call.
> The mockup link stays live if you ever want to check it out."

*[Mark as lost. Set a 90-day follow-up task.]*

---

## TIMING GUIDE

| Section | Target Time |
|---------|------------|
| Opening | 15 sec |
| Bridge | 30 sec |
| Qualify | 60 sec |
| Pitch | 45 sec |
| Close | 60 sec |
| **Total** | **~3.5 min** |

Never go over 5 minutes without consent. If they're still talking at 5 min, they're engaged — let it run.

---

## NEVER SAY

- "I'm calling to see if you'd be interested in..." (telegraphs the ask)
- "We have a special deal just for you today..." (sounds like a scam)
- "Can I ask you a few questions?" (permission-asking kills momentum)
- "We leverage cutting-edge technology..." (meaningless)
- "I understand you're busy, so I'll be quick..." (just be quick, don't narrate it)
